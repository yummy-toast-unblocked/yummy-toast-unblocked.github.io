      Instructions

      Objective:
      Score more points than the opposing team by successfully passing a ball through their goal.

      Setup:
      1. Divide players into two equal teams.
      2. Mark the playing area with two goals at opposite ends.
      3. Each team selects a goal to defend.

      Gameplay:
      1. The game starts with a jump ball at the center of the field.
      2. Players can pass, dribble, or run with the ball to advance towards the opponent's goal.
      3. To score, a player must throw or kick the ball through the opposing team's goal.
      4. After a score, the opposing team gets possession of the ball and restarts from their goal area.
      5. The game is played in two halves. The team with the most points at the end wins.

      Rules:
      1. Physical contact is limited; no tackling or aggressive moves.
      2. The ball must be passed or dribbled; no holding the ball for more than 3 seconds.
      3. Defensive players must stay an arm's length away from the ball carrier.
      4. Fouls result in a free pass for the opposing team.

      A: The team that did not touch the ball last before it went out of bounds gains possession and restarts the play from the point where the ball exited the field.

      Q: Are there any specific positions players need to play?
      A: Players can rotate and play any position, but typically teams have a mix of offensive and defensive roles.

      Q: Can players use their hands and feet to control the ball?

      Q: What is the duration of each half?

      Q: Are substitutions allowed during the game?

      Q: What constitutes a foul?

      Q: Is there an offside rule in the game?
