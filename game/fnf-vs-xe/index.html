      Instructions

      Objective:
      The objective of the game is to score points by forming words on the game board using letter tiles.

      Setup:
      1. Place the game board in the center of all players.
      2. Each player draws seven letter tiles from the tile bag and keeps them hidden from other players.
      3. Determine the starting player, usually by drawing tiles and seeing who has the letter closest to "A."

      Gameplay:
      1. The starting player forms a word using their tiles and places it on the board, ensuring one tile is on the center square.
      2. Each player takes turns forming words by adding tiles to those already on the board.
      3. Words can be formed horizontally or vertically but must be valid according to the dictionary.
      4. Players draw new tiles from the bag after their turn to always have seven tiles.
      5. If a player cannot form a word, they may exchange tiles or pass their turn.

      Scoring:
      1. Each letter has a point value. Add up the points for each letter in the word to get the word score.
      2. Special board squares can multiply letter or word scores (e.g., double letter score, triple word score).
      3. Track scores on a scorepad.

      Endgame:
      1. The game ends when all tiles have been drawn and one player uses all their tiles, or when no more valid moves can be made.
      2. Subtract any remaining tile points from each player’s score.
      3. The player with the highest score wins.

      FAQs:

      Q: Can I form a word in any direction?
      A: Words can only be formed horizontally or vertically, not diagonally.

      Q: What if I don't have any valid moves?
      A: You can either exchange some or all of your tiles or pass your turn.

      Q: Can I add letters to an existing word?
      A: Yes, you can add letters to an existing word to form a new word, as long as it is valid.

      Q: Are there any restrictions on the first word played?
      A: The first word must cover the center square of the board.

      Q: How do I handle challenges to words?
      A: If a word is challenged, consult the dictionary. If the word is invalid, the player loses their turn.

      Q: Can I use proper nouns or abbreviations?
      A: No, proper nouns and abbreviations are generally not allowed unless specified by house rules.

      Q: What happens if the tile bag is empty?
      A: Continue playing until all tiles have been used or no more valid moves can be made.

      Q: Can I skip my turn?
      A: Yes, you can choose to pass your turn if you cannot or do not wish to play a word.

      Q: How do special board squares work?
      A: Special squares multiply the score of the letter or word placed on them. Use these strategically to maximize your points.

      Q: What if two players have the same final score?
      A: In the case of a tie, the player with the highest-scoring word in a single turn usually wins. If still tied, consider it a draw or use house rules to break the tie.
