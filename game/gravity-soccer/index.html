      1. Gather a group of players (minimum 2).
      2. Divide into two teams of equal size.
      3. Set up the playing area with a clear boundary.
      4. Choose a method to start the game (e.g., coin toss, rock-paper-scissors).
      5. The team that starts possesses the ball.
      6. The goal is to score by getting the ball into the opponent's goal.
      7. Players can move the ball by running, passing, or kicking it.
      8. Players cannot use their hands or arms to touch the ball.
      9. If a player commits a foul (e.g., holding, tripping), the other team is awarded a free kick.
      10. The game continues until a set time limit or a predetermined score is reached.
      FAQs:
      - What is a foul?
      - Any action that impedes the progress of an opponent, such as holding, tripping, or pushing.
      - What happens if the ball goes out of bounds?
      - A throw-in is awarded to the team that did not touch the ball last.
      - Can players substitute during the game?
      - This depends on the specific rules of the game.
      - What is an offside?
      - A player is offside if they are in the opponent's half of the field and closer to the opponent's goal than both the ball and the second-to-last opponent.
      - How do you win?
      - The team with the most goals at the end of the game wins.
