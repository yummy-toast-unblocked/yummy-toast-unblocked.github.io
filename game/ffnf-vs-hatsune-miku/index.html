      Instructions:

      1. Gather materials: A deck of cards (standard or modified).
      2. Determine the number of players: 2-4 players.
      3. Deal cards: Each player is dealt 7 cards face down.
      4. Create a discard pile and a draw pile: Place the remaining cards face down in the center of the table. Turn over the top card to start the discard pile.
      5. Take turns: Starting with the player to the left of the dealer, each player takes their turn.
      6. Match cards: On your turn, you must try to match one of your cards to the card on top of the discard pile by either number, suit, or face card.
      7. Play cards: If you have a matching card, you play it on top of the discard pile and draw a new card from the draw pile.
      8. Draw cards: If you cannot match a card, you must draw one card from the draw pile.
      9. Go fish: If you draw a card that matches one of your face-down cards, you "go fish" by taking all the matching cards from the table and adding them to your hand.
      10. Empty hand: The first player to get rid of all their cards wins.

      FAQs:

      * What happens if the draw pile runs out? Shuffle the discard pile and use it as the new draw pile.
      * Can I play a card on my own face-down card? No, you cannot play a card on your own face-down card.
      * Can I ask another player for a card? No, you cannot ask other players for cards.
      * What is the purpose of the face cards? Face cards can be matched together regardless of suit.
      * Can I play multiple cards on the same turn? Yes, as long as they all match the top card on the discard pile.
