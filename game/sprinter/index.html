      Instructions

      Objective:
      The objective of the game is to score more points than the opposing team by throwing the ball through the hoop.

      Teams:
      The game is played with two teams, each consisting of 5 players.

      Court:
      The game is played on a rectangular court with a hoop at each end.

      Duration:
      The game consists of four quarters, each lasting 10 minutes. There is a halftime break of 15 minutes.

      Starting the Game:
      1. A jump ball is used to start the game. The referee tosses the ball into the air, and a player from each team tries to tap it to a teammate.
      2. The team that wins the jump ball gets the first possession.

      Playing the Game:
      1. Teams advance the ball by dribbling or passing.
      2. A player cannot take more than two steps without dribbling the ball (traveling).
      3. The team in possession of the ball has 24 seconds to attempt a shot (shot clock).
      4. Points are scored by successfully shooting the ball through the opponent's hoop:
      - 2 points for a field goal inside the three-point line.
      - 3 points for a field goal beyond the three-point line.
      - 1 point for each free throw.

      Defensive Play:
      1. Defenders can block shots, steal the ball, or force turnovers.
      2. Physical contact that impedes the offensive player is considered a foul.

      Fouls:
      1. Personal fouls involve illegal physical contact.
      2. Each player is allowed a certain number of personal fouls before fouling out.
      3. Team fouls lead to free throws for the opposing team once a certain limit is reached.

      Winning the Game:
      The team with the most points at the end of the fourth quarter wins. If the score is tied, an overtime period of 5 minutes is played.

      Q: How many steps can a player take without dribbling?
      A: A player cannot take more than two steps without dribbling, or it is considered traveling.

      Q: What is the shot clock?
      A: The shot clock is a 24-second timer that limits how long the offensive team has to attempt a shot.

      Q: What are personal fouls?
