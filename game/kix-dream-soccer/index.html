      Objective:
      The objective of the game is to score goals by getting the ball into the opposing team's net.

      Setup:
      1. Divide players into two equal teams.
      2. Each team should wear a different color or have some visual distinction.
      3. Set up goals at opposite ends of the playing field.
      4. Determine the length of the game (e.g., 2 halves of 30 minutes each).

      Gameplay:
      1. The game starts with a kickoff from the center of the field.
      2. Players can move the ball by kicking, passing, or dribbling.
      3. A goal is scored when the ball completely crosses the goal line between the goalposts.
      4. After a goal, the game restarts with a kickoff by the team that conceded the goal.
      5. The team with the most goals at the end of the game wins.

      Rules:
      1. Players are not allowed to use their hands or arms to touch the ball, except for the goalkeeper within the penalty area.
      2. A foul is called for actions like tripping, pushing, or kicking an opponent.
      3. A free kick is awarded to the opposing team for fouls.
      4. A penalty kick is awarded for fouls committed within the penalty area.
      5. Offside rules apply: a player cannot be ahead of the last defender when the ball is passed to them.

      FAQs:

      1. How many players are on each team?
      Typically, each team has 11 players, including the goalkeeper.

      2. What happens if the game is tied at the end of regulation time?
      The game may go into extra time or be decided by a penalty shootout, depending on the rules agreed upon before the game.

      3. Can substitutions be made?
      Yes, substitutions are allowed. The number of substitutions can vary depending on the specific rules of the game.

      4. What is a yellow card?
      A yellow card is a caution given to a player for unsportsmanlike behavior or minor infractions. Two yellow cards result in a red card and ejection from the game.

      5. What is a red card?
      A red card results in a player being sent off the field for serious misconduct or receiving two yellow cards. The team must play with one fewer player for the remainder of the game.

      6. Can the goalkeeper handle the ball at any time?
      The goalkeeper can handle the ball only within their penalty area.

      7. What is offside?
      A player is offside if they are nearer to the opponent's goal than both the ball and the second-to-last defender when the ball is passed to them, unless they are in their own half of the field.

      8. How long is a standard game?
      A standard game consists of two 45-minute halves, with a 15-minute halftime break.

      9. What is a corner kick?
      A corner kick is awarded when the ball crosses the goal line, last touched by a defending player, but does not result in a goal. The attacking team takes the kick from the nearest corner.

      10. What is a goal kick?
      A goal kick is awarded when the ball crosses the goal line, last touched by an attacking player, but does not result in a goal. The defending team takes the kick from anywhere within the goal area.
